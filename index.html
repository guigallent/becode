01 — Why BeCode's AI & Data Science Bootcamp

I want to reset and reposition my career to be more data-driven and future-proof.

For the past year and a half, I have been navigating a difficult professional transition, effectively unemployed since the start of 2026. My background is in EU public affairs, where I spent years analysing policy across a range of sectors. It is a field that is increasingly saturated, competitive, and under pressure from new tools such as AI that are reshaping how policy analysis is done.

I am someone with wide-ranging interests and greatly enjoy learning and reinventing myself. BeCode's AI & Data Science Bootcamp feels like the right opportunity to reconnect with a side of me that I left aside years ago. Before choosing international relations, I followed the scientific track at secondary school, studied basic C++ programming, and even took a year of computer engineering at university. I would like to reconcile that past with my professional experience.

I am confident that learning Python, SQL, and Power BI while building a grounding in the fundamentals of generative AI are skills I will be able to apply regardless of where my career takes me next. I am not coming to this bootcamp to explore a vague interest. I am coming with a clear intention to build something new.

02 — A year from now

I want to work as a data analyst, ideally at the intersection with public policy or in a field with strong ethical principles.

In one year, I see myself working as a data-driven policy professional, combining my experience in EU affairs with strong analytical and technical skills developed through this training. I would like to work with public datasets — such as those published by Eurostat or Comext, which track EU trade flows in granular detail — and translate them into clear, evidence-based policy recommendations, particularly in the fields of trade and climate. I am especially interested in roles within NGOs and think tanks where data can directly impact policymaking.

On a different note, I remain open to reconnecting with Japan through companies that operate at the intersection of Europe and Asia, where I could apply both my technical skills and my cross-cultural background. Organisations like NTT Data or Fujitsu, which have a strong European presence and explicit commitments to responsible AI, are the kind of environment where I could see my profile making a real contribution.

03 — Proudest moment

Finalising my violin studies in parallel to my university studies.

I completed a ten-year violin career at the conservatory while finishing my Bachelor's degree. Mornings were for university, while I was at the conservatory three afternoons a week, taking violin lessons, orchestra, chamber ensemble, and harmony classes. It was challenging, but I was motivated and organised. I not only completed my conservatory studies, but also graduated with the highest grades of my promotion for the Bachelor's.

It taught me that sustained effort across different demanding commitments is possible, as long as there is motivation.
